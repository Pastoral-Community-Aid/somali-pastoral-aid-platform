# Somali Pastoral Aid summary

## Context & challenges
- **Pastoral reliance:** 84% of Somali Regional State depends on pastoralism.
- **Marginalization:** Communities face systemic neglect and exclusion.
- **Water scarcity:** Chronic lack of clean water and sanitation, leading to migration and livestock threats.
- **Service gaps:** Few health posts, schools, and infrastructure (roads, electricity).
- **Drought vulnerability:** Heavy reliance on rain-fed resources makes them highly exposed to climate disasters.
- **Low investment:** Minimal regional and international support for livelihoods and agriculture.

## Mission & vision
- **Vision:** A resilient, self-sufficient pastoral community with equitable access to health, education, and water.
- **Mission:** Build resilience through sustainable livelihoods, infrastructure, and equitable access to essential services.
- **Approach:** Partnering with local leaders, donors, and government bodies to bridge vulnerability and long-term stability.

## Impact areas
- **Clean Water & WASH:** Deep wells, reservoirs, hygiene education.
- **Infrastructure Development:** Schools, health centers, health posts along nomadic routes.
- **Sustainable Livelihoods:** Irrigation, drought-resistant crops, livestock management.
- **Resilience Analyst Tool:** Real-time analysis of climate risks and project impact.

## Progress & engagement
- **Fundraising goal:** $50,000 for the General Resilience Fund (currently $0 committed).
- **Community updates:** Example — “Water well drilled in Korahey district!”
- **Get involved:** Donate, volunteer, or partner for collaboration and investment.
- **Transparency:** Annual reports and financial accountability emphasized.

---
**In short:** Somali Pastoral Aid focuses on tackling marginalization, water scarcity, and drought vulnerability by building resilience through clean water, infrastructure, and sustainable livelihoods, while seeking donor and partner support to reach its $50,000 resilience fund goal.
